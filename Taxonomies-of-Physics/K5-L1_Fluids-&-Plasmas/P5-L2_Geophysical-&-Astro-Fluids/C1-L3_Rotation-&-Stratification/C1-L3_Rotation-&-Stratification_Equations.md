# C1 Rotation & Stratification — Core Equations

Rotating, stratified flows exhibit balances between Coriolis, pressure-gradient, and buoyancy forces. These equations summarize key parameters and wave relations.

## Rotational Parameters
**Coriolis parameter:**

$$f = 2 \Omega \sin \phi$$

- Effective Coriolis frequency at latitude $\phi$ for planetary rotation rate $\Omega$, central to geostrophic balance.

**Rossby number:**

$$Ro = \frac{U}{f L}$$

- Compares inertial to Coriolis forces; small $Ro$ indicates rotation-dominated dynamics.

## Geostrophic & Hydrostatic Balance
**Geostrophic balance:**

$$f \mathbf{k} \times \mathbf{u}_{g} = -\frac{1}{\rho} \nabla_{h} p$$

- Links horizontal pressure gradients to geostrophic velocity $\mathbf{u}_{g}$, defining large-scale flow structure.

**Hydrostatic balance:**

$$\frac{\partial p}{\partial z} = - \rho g$$

- Vertical pressure gradient balances weight in strongly stratified atmospheres and oceans.

## Stratification & Waves
**Brunt–Väisälä frequency:**

$$N^{2} = \frac{g}{\theta_{0}} \frac{d \theta}{dz}$$

- Sets buoyancy oscillation frequency using potential temperature profile $\theta(z)$, diagnosing stability.

**Internal gravity wave dispersion:**

$$\omega^{2} = N^{2} \frac{k_{h}^{2}}{k^{2}} + f^{2} \frac{m^{2}}{k^{2}}$$

- Combines horizontal wavenumber $k_{h}$ and vertical wavenumber $m$ to describe inertia–gravity waves in rotating stratified fluids.
